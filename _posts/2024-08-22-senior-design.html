During my senior year of college, I had the opportunity to be a part of a group project where we created a mobile application that was pitched by an SMU business student. This was a great opportunity to gain experience with React Native, TypeScript and AWS, as well as working on a team of programmers, and working with a client/gathering requirements.

Background Information

Senior Design is a class offered at SMU where students partner with a sponsor to work on software over the course of their senior year. I joined the class for the second semester and was placed into a group with a project already in progress. Our sponsor was a business student at SMU who pitched a social fitness application called "Spotter".

A photo of my group, myself and our professor at the Senior Design Expo presenting our project

Technical Skills Gained

By working on this project I gained valuable skills in React Native, TypeScript and Amazon Web Services. I also learned about how to build more complex systems using a shared codebase. All of these skills have proven useful in my current projects.

Teamwork Experience

In addition to the technical knowledge I gained, I also gained valuable experience in working as a team. My team and I coordinated our Sprints using Jira and would have meetings with our sponsor frequently to adjust requirements. Working on this project was a good introduction to Agile project development, and I had a great time working with my team.
